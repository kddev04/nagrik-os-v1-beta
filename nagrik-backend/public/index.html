RTI Guide · Your Weapon
Right to Information Act, 2005 · Article 19(1)(a) · Use it fearlessly
Supreme Court of India

"Right to Information is implicit in the right to freedom of speech and expression guaranteed under Article 19(1)(a)."

01What to ask

Any information held by a public authority — PMC records, contracts, meeting minutes, inspection reports, maps, bills, employee data. Ask for copies, inspections, or certified records.

02Cost

₹10 application fee. BPL: FREE. Photocopy: ₹2/page. Pay via IPO, demand draft, or court fee stamp. Online: credit/debit card.

03Timeline

30 days standard reply. 48 hours for life/liberty. First appeal: 30 days. Second appeal to State Information Commission: 90 days.

04PIO Penalty

₹250/day fine (max ₹25,000) if PIO delays without cause. Disciplinary action possible. Cite this in your application — it works.

05File online

Maharashtra: rtionline.maharashtra.gov.in
Central: rtionline.gov.in
PMC: pmc.gov.in

06Template tips

Be specific. Number your questions. Request inspection (free). Cite Section 4 (proactive disclosure). Keep acknowledged copy always.
